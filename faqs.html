Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Courage in Common initiative.

What is the Courage in Common Ribbon?

The Courage in Common Ribbon is a multi-colored awareness ribbon designed to unite communities around veteran mental health awareness. It honors veterans, military families, and everyone committed to supporting those who have served.

What do the colors represent?

💜 Purple represents Purple Heart Cities and honors those who were wounded or killed in service to our country.

💚 Lime Green represents Veteran-Focused Mental Health Advocacy Communities — a symbol of hope, healing, and resilience.

❤️ Crimson Red Stripe represents the Marine Corps League.

🖤💛 Black and Gold Stripe represents the Suicide Awareness and Remembrance Flag Organization.

🩵 Light Blue Stripe represents AFSP's Talk Saves Lives program.

💚 Emerald Green Stripe represents the Veterans Council of Glen Cove.

How can I get involved?

There are many ways to participate: wear the ribbon, display it at your business or organization, sponsor awareness events, support local veterans, become an official community partner, or simply help spread hope one conversation at a time.

Who can become a Courage in Common Community?

Cities, towns, counties, businesses, schools, nonprofits, and veteran organizations can all participate. When a community embraces the Courage in Common mission, everyone benefits.

Where can I purchase merchandise?

Visit our Merchandise page to browse available items including ribbons, t-shirts, beanies, mugs, and travel tumblers. A portion of every sale from merchandise goes back into the Courage In Common Lapel Pin Distribution project.

How do donations work?

Donations are processed through our partner, the Veterans Council Glen Cove. Your contribution directly supports veteran wellness programs and community outreach.

Is Courage in Common a nonprofit?

Courage in Common is an initiative dedicated to promoting veteran mental health awareness. We work in partnership with established veteran organizations like the Veterans Council Glen Cove to maximize our impact.

How do I become an official community partner?

Contact us to learn about partnership opportunities. Communities that officially adopt the Courage in Common Ribbon commit to promoting veteran mental health awareness and connecting veterans with local resources.
