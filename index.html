Courage in Common™

A Symbol That Unites Those Who Stand for Our Veterans

One Ribbon. One Mission. Countless Lives Changed.

Every day, veterans and their families face invisible battles that too often go unnoticed. The Courage in Common Ribbon was created to unite communities around a simple but powerful belief:

No veteran should fight alone.

Learn More
Our Purpose

More Than a Symbol

This ribbon is more than a symbol. It is a commitment to reducing stigma, encouraging conversations about mental health, strengthening community support, and recognizing the organizations and individuals working together to improve the lives of veterans.

What It Means

What the Ribbon Represents

The Courage in Common Ribbon honors:

  • Veterans and active-duty service members
  • Military families and caregivers
  • First responders
  • Community advocates
  • Organizations committed to veteran wellness
  • Every person who believes courage is stronger when shared

It reminds us that asking for help is an act of courage — and offering support is an act of service.

Symbolism

The Meaning Behind the Colors

The ribbon was intentionally designed to recognize the organizations that stand shoulder to shoulder in support of veterans. Its colors symbolize service, sacrifice, hope, healing, and community collaboration.

Navy Blue

Service, duty, and the unwavering commitment of those who serve

Gold

Sacrifice, honor, and the value of every life served

White

Hope, healing, and the purity of community support

Green

Growth, renewal, and the journey toward wellness

Together, these colors tell a story of unity rather than division.
