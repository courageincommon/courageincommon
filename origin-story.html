Our Story

The Courage in Common Ribbon

A symbol born from the invisible wounds of war and the courage to heal together.

A Ribbon Born of Healing and Unity

After every war, American soldiers return home carrying not only visible scars but also invisible wounds — changes in mind and spirit that test understanding and compassion. These wounds are not easily seen, explained, or healed. Families and communities often watch in silence, uncertain of what to say, paralyzed by the limits of their own understanding.

Over time, society has tried to put names to these struggles:

Soldier's Heart, Battle Fatigue, Shell Shock, Combat Fatigue, Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD), and more recently, Post-Traumatic Stress Injury (PTSI)—a term that emphasizes that trauma is an injury, not a personal weakness.

Yet not all pain fits neatly under a medical term. Some experiences leave wounds we now call moral injuries — the kind that challenge one's faith in themselves, in humanity, or in the justness of the world.

Too often, our instinct is to look away. We distance ourselves from those who struggle, not because we don't care, but because we don't know what to do. But looking away has never healed anyone.

The Meaning Behind the Colors

The Courage in Common Ribbon was intentionally designed to recognize the organizations that stand shoulder to shoulder in support of veterans.

The Meaning Behind the Courage In Common Ribbon

💜 Purple

Represents Purple Heart Cities and honors those who were wounded or killed in service to our country. It symbolizes sacrifice, courage, and our commitment to never forget.

💚 Lime Green

Represents Veteran-Focused Mental Health Advocacy Communities. It is a symbol of hope, healing, resilience, and the courage to seek support before a psychological crisis occurs.

The Earned Stripes

The right side of the ribbon proudly displays earned stripes recognizing organizations that have joined the mission to support veterans.

❤️ Crimson Red Stripe

Represents the Marine Corps League, honoring its unwavering commitment to veterans, patriotism, and continued service to our communities.

🖤💛 Black and Gold Stripe

Represents the Suicide Awareness and Remembrance Flag Organization, recognizing the importance of remembering those we've lost while inspiring action to save lives.

🩵 Light Blue Stripe

Represents AFSP's Talk Saves Lives program, emphasizing that open, compassionate conversations about mental health and suicide can change—and save—lives.

💚 Emerald Green Stripe

Represents the Veterans Council of Glen Cove, whose leadership helped launch the Courage In Common movement and demonstrated how communities can unite to support veterans before they reach a psychological crisis.

The Movement Ahead

The Courage in Common Ribbon is not just a symbol to wear — it is a call to action. It reminds us that honoring veterans means more than ceremonies or parades. It means collective action. It means standing together to ensure that no one who served our nation must fight their battles alone.

Wearing or displaying the Courage in Common Ribbon is a powerful act of solidarity. It says to every veteran, every family member, every neighbor: "You are seen. You are valued. You are not alone on your journey toward wellness."

If you wish to show your understanding and appreciation for those who have served, connect with your local Marine Corps League Detachment to learn more about how your community can take part in this vital work.
